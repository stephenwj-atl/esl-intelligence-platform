# ESL Intelligence System — Core Logic (v1.0)

## System Purpose

Structure how capital is deployed under environmental uncertainty across:

- loans
- grants
- blended finance

## Core Principle

Capital moves in stages, not all at once.
Each stage requires validated reduction in risk or increase in confidence.

## FULL SYSTEM FLOW (MANDATORY — NO SKIPPING)

```
Stage 1 — Intake Screening
Stage 2 — Baseline Data Formation
Stage 3 — Risk Characterisation
Stage 4 — Decision and Capital Sequencing
Stage 5 — Deployment Control
Stage 6 — Transition Validation
Stage 7 — Risk Resolution
```

## STAGE DEFINITIONS

### Stage 1 — Intake Screening

**Purpose:** Quickly determine whether an investment or initiative should proceed to deeper evaluation.

**Produces:**
- initial risk signal
- initial confidence signal
- proceed / condition / decline

**Rule:** Do NOT invest time where risk or fit is clearly unacceptable

---

### Stage 2 — Baseline Data Formation

**Purpose:** Establish what is known, what is missing, and what must be validated.

**Produces:**
- baseline environmental profile
- data completeness
- validation gaps

**Rule:** No risk assessment without an explicit view of data quality

---

### Stage 3 — Risk Characterisation

**Purpose:** Translate baseline data into structured, confidence-weighted risk signals.

**Produces:**
- risk score
- data confidence score
- risk drivers

**Rule:** Risk without confidence is not decision-ready

---

### Stage 4 — Decision and Capital Sequencing

**Purpose:** Determine whether capital moves, how it is structured, and in what sequence.

**Produces:**
- decision (proceed / condition / defer)
- capital mode (loan / grant / blended)
- sequencing pathway

**Rule:** Uncertainty → sequence capital, not reject automatically

---

### Stage 5 — Deployment Control

**Purpose:** Control how capital is released through conditions and triggers.

**Produces:**
- covenants
- disbursement triggers
- phased capital allocation

**Rule:** No capital release without defined conditions

---

### Stage 6 — Transition Validation

**Purpose:** Verify that conditions have been met before progressing capital.

**Produces:**
- monitoring signals
- validation status
- transition approval

**Rule:** Progression requires evidence, not assumption

---

### Stage 7 — Risk Resolution

**Purpose:** Execute actions that reduce risk and improve confidence.

**Produces:**
- intervention pathways
- expected risk reduction
- confidence improvement

**Rule:** Risk must be actively reduced, not just monitored

---

## SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

```
Screen → Baseline → Understand → Decide → Control → Validate → Resolve
```

## CAPITAL TRANSLATION LAYER

**Loan**
- pricing
- insurance
- covenants

**Grant**
- impact delivery
- utilisation
- disbursement risk

**Blended**
- sequencing
- transition pathway
- capital mix

## NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES

1. No skipping stages
2. No full deployment under unresolved uncertainty
3. No decision without confidence weighting
4. No transition without validation
